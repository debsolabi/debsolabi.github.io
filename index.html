Rabbits are small mammals in the family Leporidae and in the order of Lagomorpha (along with the hare and the pika). Orcyctolagus cuniculus includes the European rabbit species and its descendents, the world's most 305 breeds of domestic rabbit. Sylvilagus includes 13 wild rabbit species, among them the 7 types of cottontail.

The European rabbit, which has been introduced on every continent except Antarctica, is familiar throughout the world as a wild prey animal and as a domesticated form of livestock and pet. With its widespread effect on ecologies and cultures, the rabbit (or bunny) is, in many areas of the world, a part of daily life-as food, clothing, a companion, and as a source of artistic inspiration.

In terminology, male rabbits are called bucks; females are called does. An older term for an adult rabbit is coney, while rabbit once referred only to the young animals. Another term for a young is bunny, though this term is often applied informally (especially by children) to rabbits generally, especially domestic ones. More recently, the term kit or kitten has been used to refer to a young rabbit.

A group of rabbits is known as a colony or nest (or, occasionally, a warren though this more commonly refers to where the rabbit live). A group of baby rabbits procuced from a single mating is referred to as a litter, and a group of domestic rabbits living together is sometimes called a herd.
